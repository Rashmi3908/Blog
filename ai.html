Artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) is an area of computer science that emphasizes the creation of intelligent machines that work and react like humans. It mainly concentrates on learning, planning, problem solving. AI was founded in 1956, and is made as an academic discipline.
The goal of AI include planning, perception and ability to move and manipulate objects, learning natural language processing.
Approaches include statistical methods, computational intelligence, and traditional symbolic AI. AI algorithms are capable of learning from data, they can enhance themselves by learning new heuristics or can themselves write other algorithms.


The field was founded on the claim that human intelligence "can be so precisely described that a machine can be made to simulate it". This raises philosophical arguments about the nature of the mind and the ethics of creating artificial beings endowed with human-like intelligence, issues which have been explored by myth, fiction and philosophy since antiquity.
